Follow the steps below to determine if you need to file a T3 Trust Income Tax and Information Return.

  1. Confirm the existence of a trust

    Does a trust exist at law?

    A trust generally exists at law when both of the following apply:

    • property is held by a trustee for the benefit of one or more beneficiaries
    • there is a clear intention to create a trust with identified property and beneficiaries or purpose

    This depends on the facts and circumstances and on the private law of the province or territory (common law or Québec civil law).

    Reference

    Consult the Enhanced reporting rules for trusts and bare trusts: Frequently asked questions for more information about determining what a trust is.

    • No: No filing required
    • Yes: Go to Step 2

  2. Identify the type of trust

    What is an express trust?

    An express trust is created intentionally by a person (the settlor), who transfers property to a trustee for the benefit of one or more beneficiaries.

    The intention to create the trust is clear, and the arrangement may be set out in writing or established through actions or agreements.

    What is a trust other than an express trust?

    These trusts are not intentionally created. They arise from the facts of a situation or are imposed by law or by a court decision.

    Examples may include trusts that result from how property is held or from legal remedies ordered by a court.

    • Express trust: Go to Step 3
    • Other than express trust: Go to Step 6a

  3. Identify the type of express trust

    What is a bare trust?

    A bare trust is generally considered a type of express trust for tax reporting purposes.

    In a bare trust, the trustee holds legal title to property but has little or no decision-making power. The beneficiary has control of the property and receives all income or benefits from it.

    The trustee typically acts only on the instructions of the beneficiary.

    • Bare trust: Go to Step 4a
    • Ordinary express trust: Go to Step 5a

  4. Reportable bare trust

    Certain bare trusts are subject to the trust reporting rules. These are described in subsection 150(1.3), subject to several exceptions described in subsection 150(1.31). These bare trusts are referred to as “reportable bare trusts,” herein.

    1. Does the 150(1.31) exception apply?

      Please refer to Income Tax Act 150(1.3).

      • Yes: No filing required
      • No: Go to Step 4b

    2. Does the 150(1.2) exception apply?

      Please refer to Income Tax Act 150(1.2).

      • Yes: Go to Step 4c
      • No: File T3RET with Schedule 15

    3. Did a taxable event occur?

      Taxable event

      A taxable event is an activity or transaction that may create income, a gain, or another amount that must be reported for tax purposes.

      • Yes: File T3RET without Schedule 15
      • No: No filing required
